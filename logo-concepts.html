The Monument (Refined). Off-white limestone arch and columns with visible masonry blocks throughout the curve. The columns are styled with classical fluting (vertical grooves) and serif-like capitals and bases — inspired by Roman column proportions. Individual stones are separated by radial joint lines with a horizontal ring course for depth. The golden keystone at the crown is the only coloured element — it pops against the pale stone. Font upgraded to Cormorant Garamond (elegant serif) to match the classical architecture.

The Modern Arch. Same arch DNA but cleaner — one continuous thick shape with smooth curves and no separate pillars. The golden keystone is wider and more prominent than Concept 1, sitting boldly at the crown with diagonal separation lines where it meets the arch. The blue gradient flows from deep navy at the base through royal blue up to cyan near the top. Minimal, contemporary, but still clearly an arch.

The Gateway. Wider and more open than the others — feels like walking through a grand entrance. The keystone is the widest of all 5 concepts with multiple facet lines for a faceted gem-like quality. Subtle warm glow inside the opening. The gradient sweeps from deep navy at the base corners through royal blue up to cyan at the top. The base line grounds the whole thing. This is the "freedom doorway" — your old life on one side, your new free time on the other.

The Crowned Arch. Just the top half of the arch — cropped at the shoulders. This makes it more compact and logo-shaped (closer to a badge or icon). The keystone is front and centre with a diamond sparkle at its apex. More voussoir lines than the other concepts give it proper masonry texture. The gradient runs the full blue-to-cyan spectrum across the curve. Works well at small sizes because the legs don't crowd the shape — perfect for favicons and social avatars.

The Dimensional. Same arch but with 3D perspective — a second layer behind creates depth, with connecting faces visible on the sides and top. The keystone has a visible top face (lighter gold) giving it a physical, tangible quality. This is the most premium-feeling — like an actual stone arch casting a shadow. The depth effect makes it feel substantial and real. Works especially well on dark backgrounds where the shadow and layering really pop.
01 — Monument Classic Roman arch. Thick pillars, heavy stone, pillar caps. Most traditional and authoritative.
